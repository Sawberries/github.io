\nI have been asked a lot of questions lately about Stanford’s online course offerings and why somebody would choose them over myriad of options online. This is my attempt to bundle them together to help a broader audience.<\/p>\n
\n<\/a><\/div>\n

1. How do you choose classes?<\/b>
\nIt depends on your goals and interests. Here are some questions to ask.<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Goal:\u00a0<\/li>\n
      \n
    • What do you want to achieve out of a particular course?\u00a0<\/li>\n
    • Are you learning for fun or do you want to apply the knowledge to build something?<\/li>\n
    • Do you want to extend\/switch careers to become a Deep Learning practitioner?<\/li>\n
    • Do you think these tools will help you solve a real-life problem?<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
    • Interests:<\/li>\n
        \n
      • Read through the course page, find out the topics they cover and search for applications\/projects related to it. Do these topics interest you? Here is my review<\/a> on some of the classes to help you get started.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n<\/ul>\n

        2. Reference Materials for classes?<\/b><\/p>\n

          \n
        • Most classes at Stanford are self-contained but you are welcomed to read and research resources, there is tons of literature on most topics as it’s an active field of research.\u00a0<\/li>\n
        • Teaching staff usually provides references for each topic which help you consolidate concepts.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

          3. Are there any forums to discuss doubts? Is there any form of Mentorship provided?<\/b><\/p>\n

            \n
          • There are Course Assistants for every class and there is also a forum setup per class where you can discuss doubts and ask questions to help solidify your understanding.\u00a0<\/li>\n
          • You are also assigned a Mentor for your project which is one of the course assistants for the class. They help give feedback and suggestions to help you navigate and sketch out scope of a project.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
            \n4. What can you get out of a class project?<\/b><\/div>\n

            <\/p>\n

              \n
            • A class project can be an open research problem, your attempt at reproducing ideas presented in a paper or simply applying fundamentals to a real-life problem. This is one of the most interesting parts of the courses which helps you exercise what you have learnt and even explore beyond the class.\u00a0<\/li>\n
            • Choosing challenging course projects helps you explore and become better at applying concepts and figure out how to use these tools in real-life problems.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

              5. What Programming Language is preferred?<\/b><\/p>\n

                \n
              • Most classes require you to code in Python.\u00a0<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

                6. Why is it still worth taking the course when you have online courses at a much lower cost?<\/b><\/p>\n

                  \n
                • These are actual “Graduate” courses taken by students at Stanford. I have found them to be quite challenging but at the same time rewarding in ways that broaden your horizon and knowledge with State-of-the-Art (SoTA) literature and techniques.\u00a0<\/li>\n
                • I would suggest using other online courses as basics to build upon with these Graduate level classes.\u00a0<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n


                  <\/b>
                  \n7. Why one shouldn’t one be\u00a0<\/b>judgmental<\/b>\u00a0about this course since it is a “Graduate Certificate” unlike a full time Master’s course?<\/b><\/p>\n

                    \n
                  • Graduate level courses are more abstract compared to their undergraduate counterparts. Again, It depends on what your goals are, if you have a Master’s you can update yourself with these classes.\u00a0<\/li>\n
                  • On the other hand, my understanding is that the same courses can be taken as part of an online Master’s program.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
