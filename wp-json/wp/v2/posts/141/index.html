\nI have been enrolled at Stanford and have been taking their courses online. Here are my few cents on the ones I have taken so far.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

\n<\/a><\/div>\n

CS224n – Natural Language Processing with Deep Learning (Prof. Manning)<\/b><\/p>\n

    \n
  • Difficulty: 4\/5 (Moderate)<\/li>\n
  • What to expect:\u00a0<\/li>\n
      \n
    • Get exposed to State-of-the-Art (SoTA) Deep Learning techniques applied to NLP. Key topics:\u00a0<\/li>\n
        \n
      • Question and Answering<\/li>\n
      • Text Summarization<\/li>\n
      • Parts of Speech tagging<\/li>\n
      • Sequence-to-Sequence models<\/li>\n
      • Transformers<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
      • Gives you a very good overview of where NLP is headed, homeworks<\/a> are challenging but allow you to implement latest neural architectures to solve various language problems.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
      • My class project: BertQA<\/a>\u00a0(99* stars on github) – Won Best Project Award in the class<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
        \n<\/div>\n

        CS231n –\u00a0Convolutional Neural Networks for Visual Recognition (Prof. Li and Justin Johnson)<\/b><\/p>\n

        \n<\/p>\n
          \n
        • Difficulty: 4\/5 (Moderate)<\/li>\n
        • What to expect:\u00a0<\/li>\n
            \n
          • Extensive overview of latest trends in Computer Vision techniques across different domains and applications –\u00a0<\/li>\n
              \n
            • Discriminative models<\/li>\n
            • Unsupervised techniques<\/li>\n
            • Neural Architecture layers and intutions\u00a0<\/li>\n
            • Segmentation<\/li>\n
            • Generative Techniques<\/li>\n
            • Style Transfer<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
            • Homeworks are the best part of the class which allow you to implement a variety of Neural Layers and get in-depth intuition of how deep learning actually works.<\/li>\n
            • I would suggest some familiarity with matrix calculus and probability<\/i> for this course.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
            • My class project:\u00a0Spatio-Temporal Adversarial Video Super Resolution<\/a><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
              \n
              <\/b><\/div>\n
              \nCS221 – Artificial Intelligence – Principles and Techniques (Prof. Liang and Prof. Sadigh)<\/b><\/div>\n
              \n
                \n
              • Difficulty: 4.5\/5 (Heavy)<\/li>\n
              • What to expect:\u00a0<\/li>\n
                  \n
                • This is one of the most “dense” classes I have come across at Stanford. The nature of the class is such that it is trying to fit in this huge umbrella of AI topics within a quarter – which is what makes it challenging. Topics include –\u00a0<\/li>\n
                    \n
                  • Search<\/li>\n
                  • Markov Decision Process<\/li>\n
                  • Reinforcement Learning (RL)<\/li>\n
                  • Adversarial Games<\/li>\n
                  • Constraint Satisfaction problems<\/li>\n
                  • Bayesian Networks (BN)<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
                  • Amongst these, Reinforcement Learning and Bayesian Networks are conceptually heavy topics which require some extra effort.<\/li>\n
                  • That being said, the topics are interesting and makes you appreciate the latest trends in AI and draw parallels from traditional techniques.\u00a0<\/li>\n
                  • Homeworks are weekly and can take time but are fun for the most part! You build your own Pacman game.\u00a0<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

                    • Work in progress (to be updated shortly)<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n<\/ul>\n
